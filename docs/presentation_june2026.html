What Happened This Week

On the night of June 7, we ran an emergency reprice of all 265 listings. The store had gone silent — zero sales — because the pricing was 2 to 4.5 times the actual eBay sell-through price. The root cause was over-reliance on CollX platform averages, which were running hot due to outlier sales inflating the numbers.

A three-expert panel was convened: an eBay pricing specialist, a card show dealer with street-level market knowledge, and a data analyst who dissected the CollX methodology. Their consensus: cut everything to market. 265 listings repriced in one operation. Sales began arriving the same evening.

Key insight from the panel: CollX prices were running 2-4.5x actual eBay sell-through for bulk singles. The Will Howard Silver Prizm RC was listed at $18 against a real median of $3.99 — a 4.5x overinflation. The Tim Shough Chrome RC was listed at $46 when real comps showed $6-8. These were systematic data errors, not one-offs.

What the Reprice Looked Like

  • 220 bulk singles: all dropped to $1.99 flat
  • 45 named items: specific drops with Best Offer enabled (Warren Moon lot $47.99 → $24.99, Cam Ward RC $27.99 → $9.99)
  • Garrett Nussmeier Wild Card RC 1/2: raised from $5.99 to $14.99 — the panel flagged it was being undersold as a 1-of-2 card
  • Promoted listings spend: cut from $21/month to $3.54 — 83% reduction, no impact on conversion

Executive Q&A

CFO — Financial Questions

Q1: What is the actual landed cost of goods sold? Do we have a defensible gross margin?

Response: Fair challenge. The inventory.csv tracks acquired_price per card. For the June 7 reprice cohort, the majority of bulk singles came from lot purchases averaging $0.15-0.30/card. At $1.99 list and 13.25% FVF plus $0.60 USPS Ground Advantage per single, the contribution margin on a single-card sale is approximately $0.67-0.90. Multi-card orders are where it becomes profitable — 5 cards in one PWE at $9.95 nets roughly $7.60 after all costs. The immediate dashboard fix: surface acquired_price from inventory.csv so we can show real-time gross margin per listing. That is on the build list.

Q2: Mahomes flip velocity — is that per-seller or market-wide, and what is days-to-sell?

Response: Correct to push on this. The 31/month is eBay market-wide sold velocity for that specific card and condition. Per-seller capture rate at our size is likely 2-4 sales per month per card type. At $140 net and 3 sales per month per flip SKU, that is $420/month from one Mahomes variant. With 5 variants in rotation, $2,100/month. The days-to-sell for Mahomes Prizm parallels in the $20-40 range is consistently 7-21 days based on sold history. We will add a days-to-sell estimate to the Buy Radar cards on the dashboard.

Q3: Why is Best Offer only on 39 of 263 listings?

Response: It was zero as of June 6. We ran best_offer_agent --apply on June 7 and enabled it on all 39 eligible listings (priced $5+ with margin to negotiate). The remaining 224 listings are $1.99 bulk — Best Offer on a $1.99 card does not make economic sense. The conversion delta data will arrive in the next orders pull. Adding a BO conversion rate metric to the dashboard is the right call.

The path to $5k/month is not selling more $1.99 cards — it is shifting the mix toward these three categories.
