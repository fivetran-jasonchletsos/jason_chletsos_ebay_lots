Where these prices come from

A straight answer, while this is still a rough estimate

Every dollar amount on The Sewer — on each card and in the suggested lot prices — comes from the same AI that reads the photo when a card gets scanned. It's making an educated guess based on what similar-looking cards typically sell for. It is not looking up a real eBay sold listing or a live price guide.

For common modern sports and Pokemon cards, that guess is usually a reasonable ballpark. It gets much shakier for anything the AI doesn't have good comparison data for — and that includes non-sport, pop-culture card lines like Panini Prizm's music and entertainment sets.

Case in point: the two Charlie Watts (Panini Prizm, Rolling Stones) cards on the live board are priced at $15 and $20. Amanda found real eBay lots of this same set selling for as little as $5. I checked the same live comp source used to price eBay lots for the main store (SportsCardsPro) — it has zero real data for this set. Every result it returns is an unrelated sports card that happens to share a word, not an actual match. So that $15/$20 is a pure AI guess with nothing real behind it, and Amanda's real-world number is almost certainly closer to the truth.

Bottom line: treat every price here as a rough starting point, not a final answer — especially for anything that isn't a mainstream sports or Pokemon card. Before actually listing a lot, it's worth a quick check of real, recent sold listings on eBay for that exact set.

This is a known limitation, not a bug — JC may wire up real live comps for more product lines down the road. For now, if a price looks off, it probably is; trust what you're actually seeing sell over what's printed on the card page.
