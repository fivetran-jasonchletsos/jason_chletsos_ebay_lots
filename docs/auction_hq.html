The 2-week runway

Days 1-2 — Apply

Apply to sell on Whatnot (app → "+ Sell" → Get Started). They want your legal name matching your ID, your eBay link, category = sports cards, and a 30-60 sec video showing inventory — that video is what gets you approved fast. Also start the eBay Live seller interest form (invite-gated, so get in the queue now; your harpua2001 store helps).

Days 2-5 — Get verified + gear up

Finish ID/KYC + link a bank for payouts. Order the starter gear (see Gear tab — ~$120 phone rig gets you live; ~$450 is the sweet spot). Set up the table.

Days 5-9 — Prep inventory + do a PRIVATE test

Pull a themed batch (start with the glut: Prizm/Select $1-10 singles). Sort in run order. Run a private practice stream to nobody — get the camera/audio/pacing reps in. This is the single best confidence builder.

Days 10-14 — Schedule + go live

Schedule your first show 24-48h ahead (Thu-Sat, 7-10 PM ET), post a teaser on IG/TikTok, line up a giveaway. Then run it. First show ≈ 60-90 min, 30-50 cheap cards, one giveaway in the first 5 minutes.

Reality check (so nobody quits in month 1): your first shows will have single-digit viewers and some cards will sell for $1. That's normal — ~80% of new sellers quit in the first two months. The winners commit to 2-4 fixed shows a week for 3 months. You're building an audience and reps, not a paycheck, in month one.

The Playbook

How the money actually works, so you don't grind for nothing.

The #1 thing: combined shipping is the whole engine

Individually, cheap singles lose money — Whatnot's flat $0.30 per order plus 8% + 2.9% eats a $1-3 card alive. But when one buyer wins several lots in your show, they combine into one order, one label, one $0.30. That's what turns a money-losing $1 single into a profitable basket.

Your entire job is getting each buyer to win multiple lots. Sell fast, keep them watching, run bins.

DO Singles-auction nights, themed by team/player/era. Open with cheap $1-5 bangers to warm the room, bring the good cards out in the second half.

The crew (one each)

HOST

The Face

Runs the mic + energy. Presents every card, calls the auction, banters with chat, hypes what's next. Never stops to hunt cards or type.

RUNNER

The Hands

Feeds the next card to the host in run order, clears sold cards, keeps supplies stocked, pre-sleeves during slow moments. Keeps tempo so the host never waits.

PACKER / MOD

The Eyes

Works the chat (greet by name, answer "what's the serial?"), logs every sale to a buyer, and starts packing during the show. Highest-ROI role — protects the shipping SLA.

Research says: packer first (shipping is the throughput ceiling), moderator second (chat buyers spend ~2x), one consistent host. Rotate who hosts as everyone gets comfortable.

Taxes & business (do this from day one)

Treat it as a business, not a hobby (hobby expenses aren't deductible). Consider an LLC + EIN + a dedicated bank account so it's not tied to one person's SSN.

  • Track cost basis from card #1 (a shared ledger) — you're taxed on the gain, and Whatnot's 1099-K reports gross including shipping. No basis = IRS can tax the whole sale.
  • Federal 1099-K: only over $20,000 AND 200+ transactions (2026). But state thresholds are lower — NJ is $1,000, no transaction minimum.
  • Sales tax: Whatnot collects & remits it — you don't touch it.
  • Deduct platform fees, processing, shipping, supplies. Pull each show's Whatnot Seller Statement — it itemizes your deductible fees.
  • One person owns quarterly estimated taxes (1040-ES). Net profit owes ~15.3% self-employment tax on top of income tax.

Not tax advice — worth a quick chat with a CPA once you're consistently selling.

The table layout

One line: sorted bins (host's dominant hand) → overhead "hot zone" on a dark mat (center, two lights at 10 & 2 o'clock) → sleeves/toploaders (near edge) → "sold" tray (far side) → packing station beside/behind.

Dark mat kills holo glare. Never one light straight overhead. Face cam at eye level for reactions.

Packing station (fast fulfillment)

Penny sleeves → toploader (seal with painter's tape, never Scotch) → cardboard sandwich + team bag → PWE under ~$20 / bubble mailer with tracking $20+ → weigh → thermal label. Batch identical envelopes. Ship within 24-48h and get the drop-off scan (as of Mar 2026 you eat the refund on late unscanned orders).

Software

Phone-only or two-device (one streams, one controls) = the native Whatnot app, no computer. Face + overhead + branded overlays = OBS on a laptop (use your phone as the product cam inside it — phone cams beat webcams). Don't jump to OBS until you're comfortable going live.
