The AI feature that turns my cheap singles into sellable lots.

I run an eBay sports-card store solo. Half the cards in my inventory are worth more in eBay fees than they earn at the listed price. So I built an AI agent that finds those sub-economic cards and proposes themed bundles that DO sell. Built on top of CollX (Philadelphia neighbors of mine) for the identification and pricing layer.

How it actually works

The Lot Picker is a Python agent that runs against my unlisted CollX inventory. Three steps, all heuristic — no LLM required in v1.

  • Candidate selection. Pull every card from my CollX collection where the market value is empty or below $3.00 AND the card is not already live on eBay. Tonight: 93 cards out of a 100-ish unlisted pool.
  • Five grouping passes. Cards run through five themed groupings in order — by NFL team, by player (multiple parallels of one star), by set / year (set-builders), by entire rookie class, and by parallel type (shiny / Prizm / Refractor). Same card can only land in one lot.
  • Pricing. Bulk lots price at the max of 60% of the sum of CollX market values, $1.50 per card, or a $9.99 floor. Focused lots (one player, one parallel type) price tighter at 75% of sum, because the theme adds value beyond the raw sum.

The CollX layer — why this works at all

Built on CollX · Philadelphia

CollX is the identification and pricing engine I rely on every day.

I scan a card in the CollX app on my phone. CollX identifies the card, returns the market value, stores the photo, tracks it in my collection. My pipeline imports the CollX export and layers on the sell-side logic — eBay listing automation, sold-state reconciliation, and the Lot Picker. CollX is the moat I'm not trying to rebuild. They're a Philadelphia-local company doing the hard problem of card identification at scale.

The safety pattern

The Lot Picker never pushes to eBay on its own. It runs in dry-run mode by default, writes a proposal JSON, renders a printable PDF picking list (A-Z by player last name, so I can walk my binder in order), and waits for me to physically pull each card and approve the lot. Only then does push_lots_to_ebay.py fire — and only with an explicit typed confirmation. Three gates between the agent and a real eBay listing.
