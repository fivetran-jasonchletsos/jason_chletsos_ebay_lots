Getting You Hooked Up To eBay

This page walks you through getting three things from eBay's website. Once Jason has them, he can turn on the button that lets you pick cards here and send them straight to eBay to sell.

← Back to the collection
Quick heads up: the page you screenshotted last time said "SBX" on it — that's eBay's practice/test mode, kind of like a demo account. It looks identical to the real thing but nothing posted through it would actually show up for real buyers. The steps below get you the real ("Production") version instead.
Part 1 · Turn on the real keys
Right now your eBay developer page is set to "Sandbox" (practice mode). We need to switch it to "Production" (the real thing).
Go back to developer.ebay.com/my/keys — the same page you were on before.
Near the top of that page, find a link that says something like "Request a Production Keyset" (it may just say "Request another key set"). Click it.
eBay might ask you a couple of questions to confirm it's really you — that's normal, just answer them honestly.
You'll land on a new page showing an App ID, Dev ID, and Cert ID — same three labels as before. Check that the App ID does NOT have "SBX" in it anywhere this time.
Screenshot that whole page (all three IDs) and text or email it to Jason.

Part 2 · Make sure you can sell on eBay
This is separate from the developer stuff above — it's just your regular eBay account, the kind anyone uses to sell things.
Go to ebay.com and log in with the eBay account you actually want listings to come from.
Click "Sell" in the top corner and start (don't finish) a listing. If it lets you get partway through, your account is good to go — you can close out, we don't need to actually post anything by hand.
Part 3 · Get the permission slip
This last piece is what actually lets our app post listings using your eBay account, instead of just knowing the app exists.
Back on the same eBay developer page (now showing your Production keys), find a link called "User Tokens" and click it.
If it asks you to fill in a business-y field like a "privacy policy" web address, don't stress about it — put in any website address (even a link to this Eagles Nest page works), or just text Jason and he'll fill in that one box with you.
You'll get to a page with a "Sign In" button. Sign in with the same real eBay account from Part 2.
It'll ask if you want to allow the app to access your account — click "I Agree" / "Allow".
You'll land on a page with one long string of letters and numbers — that's the token. Screenshot that whole page and send it to Jason too.
