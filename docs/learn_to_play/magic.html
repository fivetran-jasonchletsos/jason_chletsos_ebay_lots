Magic: The Gathering for beginners

Magic has more moving parts than Pokémon, but you don't need all of them for a fun first game. This covers the kitchen-table version — enough to actually play, with the deeper rules waiting for when you want them.

The goal

Each player starts at 20 life. Deal damage with creatures and spells to bring your opponent to 0 or below and you win. (There are a couple of alternate ways to lose — drawing from an empty library, or certain rare card effects — but life total is the one that matters for your first games.)

The five colors

Every card belongs to one or more of 5 colors (or none, for "colorless" cards like most Artifacts). Colors aren't teams you're locked into — they're flavors of strategy, and most decks mix 1-2.

White
Teamwork, small efficient creatures, protection
Blue
Card draw, countering spells, tricky plays
Black
Removal, sacrifice, drain life for value
Red
Aggression, direct damage, fast decks
Green
Big creatures, extra mana, raw size

What's in a deck

Constructed (regular play): minimum 60 cards, no more than 4 copies of any card (basic Lands excepted). Roughly 40% of your deck should be Lands — about 17 out of 60.

Limited (Draft/Sealed) — easiest way to start with what you're pulling: just 40 cards minimum, built only from packs you've opened. This is the natural on-ramp for a family that's collecting from packs rather than buying full playsets.

  • Lands — tap for mana. Basic Lands (Plains=White, Island=Blue, Swamp=Black, Mountain=Red, Forest=Green) are the mana engine of every deck.
  • Creatures — attack and block. Have Power (damage dealt) and Toughness (damage they can take).
  • Instants / Sorceries — one-time effects. Sorceries only on your own main phase; Instants any time, including on your opponent's turn.
  • Artifacts / Enchantments — stick around and provide an ongoing effect.
  • Planeswalkers — powerful permanents with their own abilities and loyalty counters, can be attacked like a player.

How a turn works

1. Untap2. Upkeep3. Draw 4. Main Phase 15. Combat6. Main Phase 27. End step

In plain terms: untap everything you had tapped, draw a card, play a Land (1 per turn) and any creatures/spells you can afford, then decide whether to attack. If you attack, your opponent chooses which of their untapped creatures block. Damage happens, then you get a second main phase to play more spells before passing the turn.

Mana cost: the symbols in a card's top-right corner. Tap that many matching Lands to pay for it. A card costing 2RR needs 2 of any land plus 2 Mountains.

Terms you'll actually hear

Tap / UntapTurning a card sideways to use it (mana, attacking); it untaps next turn.
Mana / CMCThe colored energy Lands produce; a card's total cost is its "converted mana cost."
Summoning sicknessA creature can't attack or tap for an ability the turn it enters play.
The stackWhen spells are cast in response to each other, the most recent one resolves first — like a stack of plates.
FlyingCan only be blocked by creatures with Flying or Reach.
First strike / DeathtouchFirst strike deals combat damage before normal creatures; Deathtouch destroys anything it damages.
TrampleExcess combat damage past a blocker spills over to the defending player.
RemovalAny spell whose job is to destroy, exile, or otherwise get rid of a creature.
MulliganShuffle a bad opening hand back in and draw a new 7 (modern rule: draw 7, put 1 back for each mulligan taken).
Commander / EDHA popular multiplayer format — 100-card deck, 40 life, built around one legendary creature. Good format once all 4 of you want to play at one table.

Your first game — checklist

  • Shuffle your deck (60-card constructed, or 40-card if playing Limited from your own packs).
  • Both players draw an opening hand of 7 cards; mulligan if it's unplayable (no lands, or all lands).
  • Flip a coin — winner chooses who goes first. The player going first skips their very first draw step.
  • Each turn: untap, draw, play a land if you have one, cast what you can afford, decide whether to attack, pass.
  • Track life total starting at 20 each (dice, a phone notes app, or paper all work fine).
  • First player to 0 life (or an agreed alternate loss condition) loses.

Building your first deck from what you've already pulled

Since you're pulling from packs rather than buying singles, start in Limited mode — build straight from what's actually in the binder, 40 cards minimum.

  1. Sort by color. Spread out your creatures and spells and see which 1-2 colors you have the most playable cards in.
  2. Pick your 2 colors. Two-color decks are the sweet spot for beginners — enough variety, not so much you're stuck holding uncastable cards.
  3. Count your creatures. Aim for roughly 15-17 creatures plus a handful of removal/tricks in your 40, the rest lands.
  4. Add basic Lands to hit ~17 lands (for 40 cards, more like 16-17; scale up for 60). Split lands to roughly match how many cards need each color.
  5. Playtest solo — deal yourself 7 cards, "play" a few turns against nobody, and see if the deck feels smooth before your first real match.

Since there are 4 of you: Magic is normally 1v1, so pair off and rotate, or once everyone knows the basics, try a free-for-all multiplayer game (everyone starts at 20 life, last player standing wins) — it's slower but genuinely built for more than 2 players at once.
